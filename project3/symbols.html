Meaning Behind the Symbols

There are many symbols that are borrowed from ancient Pagan symbolism. Many symbols are also related to Christianity.

Wheat/Grain

Wheat and Grain symbolize good health and wishes for a bountiful harvest. Since the eggs are traditionally made around Easter it is a wish for the upcoming gardening and harvest season. Wheat also is something that is a part of the traditional Ukrainian dish, Kutia which is made of wheat, milk, poppy seeds, and honey. Kutia is one of the 12 dishes in the Ukrainian Christmas Eve Supper.

Roses

Roses represent different things based on their color. For example, red roses represent deep emotions. Yellow roses represent caring and friendship. In general, they each have some sort of emotion attached to it. The color effects exactly what the rose means. In general, Flowers are generally thought to be special gifts from God.

Stars

This is a symbol that is taken from Pagan culture, since the pysanky were made in the spring, in the Pagan time, the stars are supposed to represent the celebration of the return of the sun. Sometimes suns and stars may look like roses; however, roses normally keep some sort of plant symbols with it.

Fish

Fish is a symbol of Christianity. In Greek, the word "Ichthys" both means fish and is an acronym that stands for "Jesus Christ Son of God Savior". The fish also symbolizes sacrifice.

Triangles

Triangles are all about the power of three. In the Christian belief, a triangle can represent the Father, Son and Holy Spirit. A triangle with a circle in the middle of it represents the eye of God.

Deer, Horses

Animals in general may represent wishes for healthy livestock for the year. Deer and Horses specifically may be symbols for wishes of prosperity and wealth.

Basket weaves

The weave pattern may represent a net or a sieve in addition to a basket. If it is used in the net sense, it symbolizes a fishermen's net to cast a net and collect fish or people into Christianity. When used in the basket sense, it can represent the meaning of knowledge. Used in the sieve sense, it separates good and bad.

Meaning Behind the Dye Colors

White

White is a symbol of purity and innocence.

Yellow

Yellow is commonly used with the Sun and the Stars. It symbolizes wisdom, warmth and the continuation of family. It can also be a symbol for the harvest.

Orange

Orange is another symbol commonly used with the Sun and Stars. It symbolizes endurance, everlasting sun, strength and ambition.

Green

Green was thought to be the first sign of spring, like the buds on the trees start to form and stems start to appear in the dirt. It is a sign of hope and freshness. It is also associated with wealth.

Red

The color red before artificial dyes used to be made from Red Beet juice. Red represents hapiness, passion, blood, fire, and passion.

Blue

You don't see a lot of blue used on a traditional pysanky. It symbolizes a wishes for good health; it also represents sky, air, truth and fidelity.

Black

Black is absolute. It is also the darkest color you can dye an egg. Once you go to black you can never get another color. It may represent the darkness before light, eternity, or even the center of the Earth. It also has a large association with the remembrance of those who have passed.

Purple

Purple symbolizes royalty, faith and trust. Sometimes when is dyed with red and then blue depending on the dye you are using it will make purple instead of the blue you would like it to be.

Brown

An egg may start out as being brown instead of white. Regardless, brown represents the earth, harvest and generosity.
